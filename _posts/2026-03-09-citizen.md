## I passed my U.S. citizenship interview and test on April 14th, 2026!

## Full Timeline:

* **December 9, 2025**: Filed my N-400 application online.

* **March 9, 2026**: Received the interview schedule notice for April 14.

* **April 14, 2026**: Visited the USCIS office and received official confirmation that I passed both the interview and the exams.

Although the USCIS website estimated an average processing time of 7 to 8 months, it only took 4 months from filing to passing.

## More Strict Eligibility
According to recent news, individuals with criminal records are being approached and detained by ICE agents during their green card or citizenship interviews. We have entered an era where only those who have never committed even minor offenses can apply for permanent residency or citizenship with peace of mind.

Although I have never had any criminal record in both countries, I still found myself unnecessarily anxious during the citizenship process—for example, I would try not to even slightly exceed the speed limit, like staying under 40 mph in a 45 mph zone, and living each day with that kind of constant caution and stress.

## Reasons for the Expedited Process and Key Advice (My personal take):
* Prevent delays caused by Requests for Evidence (RFE) by preparing documents thoroughly.
* Ensure accuracy and consistency through meticulous proofreading.
* Maintain integrity as it is paramount during the interview.
* I believe my military commendations, including awards for exemplary conduct and the apprehension of a sexual offender, left a very positive impression.
* My academic focus M.S. in STEM served as a significant advantage.
* My placement on the Dean’s List at Ohio State University and my record of winning scholarships demonstrated my character and contributions.

## 20 Random Sample Questions
Below are 20 random sample questions. If you are a U.S. born citizen, see if you can correctly answer some of them for fun

1. What is the supreme law of the land? 
2. How many amendments does the U.S. Constitution have? 
3. What is the economic system of the United States? 
4. Name one power of the U.S. Congress. 
5. What is the name of the Speaker of the House of Representatives now? 
6. The President of the United States is elected for how many years? 
7. What is the name of the President of the United States now? 
8. What is the name of the Vice President of the United States now? 
9. Who vetoes bills? 
10. What is the highest court in the United States? 
11. Who is the governor of your state now? 
12. What do we show loyalty to when we say the Pledge of Allegiance? 
13. Who lived in America before the Europeans arrived? 
14. Who wrote the Declaration of Independence? 
15. George Washington is famous for many things. Name one. 
16. Abraham Lincoln is famous for many things. Name one. 
17. What major event happened on September 11, 2001 in the United States? 
18. Why does the flag have 13 stripes? 
19. Name one thing the U.S. Constitution does. 
20. What is the rule of law? 

## Answers for questions above
1. (U.S.) Constitution
2. Twenty-seven (27)
3. Capitalism
4. Writes laws
5. Visit uscis.gov/citizenship/testupdates for the name of the Speaker of the House of Representatives.
6. Four (4) years
7. Visit uscis.gov/citizenship/testupdates for the name of the President of the United States.
8. Visit uscis.gov/citizenship/testupdates for the name of the Vice President of the United States.
9. The President (of the United States)
10. Supreme Court
11. Answers will vary.
12. The United States
13. American Indians
14. (Thomas) Jefferson
15. First president of the United States
16. Freed the slaves (Emancipation Proclamation)
17. Terrorists attacked the United States
18. Because there were 13 original colonies
19. Forms the government
20. Everyone must follow the law
